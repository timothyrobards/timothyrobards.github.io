ABOUT US

The year was 1929. Myer Gurman had come to Canada from Europe with little money and seven children to feed. Amidst the hardships of a stock market crash, Myer had his own dream. He opened his own small business on Coloniale street in Montreal and began making caps with his wife Rachel by his side.

His business slowly grew, and in 1952 his son Jack joined the company. Following in his father's footsteps Jack expanded JayGur Manufacturing and in addition to making caps the company now made aprons and children's shirts. With his wife Zelda helping him, they worked day and night to build up the company which was now located on St. Lawrence Street.

During the next two decades, Jack saw the opportunites to bring affordable goods to Canada through importing. The company evolved into JayGur Imports, with the goal of bringing the best quality products to fellow Canadians at the most affordable prices. Jack frequently travelled across Canada's vast distances to bring quality goods to even the most remote regions.

As the years went by Jack and Zelda had three children: a daughter Rickie and twin sons Marvin and Jonathan. The parents worked tirelessly to build the business. In 1978 their sons joined the company. Their youth and energy brought a new vision of global approach to the business. Jonathan and Marvin set out to develop product programs that would meet the mass market needs of retailers and consumers across North America.

In 1992 the company became known as JayGur International. Without losing the lessons of hard work, craftmanship and quality that their father and grandfather passed on through generations, the Gurman brothers developed the business into an international powerhouse.

They created new state of the art offices, with a massive design team, and yet still had the loyalty of many of the original employees their father had hired. Mixing modern know-how with good old-fashioned expertise, JayGur International today comprises three major divisions, Jonathan G, Gurilla Biscuit Entertainment, and Starter Apparel, each with its own specialty, each one committed to do the very best in that product range.

In addition, the company developed a strong private label program. "We're an item orientated business," explains company president Jonathan Gurman. "We focus on creating the best possible apparel for the entire family, with a commitment to quality for the mass market that you can spot anywhere." At JayGur, it's this philosophy that sets them apart.

Marketing on an international basis meant development across North America and sourcing the world for the very best in production methods. A key strength is the company's expertise in bringing goods to the market, from the factories overseas and containers on shipping vessels, to the fleet of JayGar trucks which supply over 500 tons of merchandise each year. Today JayGur International controls over 30 factories across Asia and has fully staffed offices in several key regions throughout the far east, including their Hong Kong and Taipei based Asian corporate offices.

Today, the international aspects of JayGur International have made it a leader in the mass market, with Jonathan G denims and sportswear, Gurilla Biscuit Entertainment division of licenced accessories, and the Starter Apparel brand of activewear. JayGur International is today one of the largest and most efficiently run apparel and accessory companies in North America.

As a company that has clothed generations of children through the decades, Jonathan and Marvin have become powerful advocates of children's research causes at hospitals such as the Montreal Children's Hospital, the Saint-Justine's Hospital as well as the Elizabeth Glazer Pedatric Aids Foundation, in a spirit to "give back" to the children.
